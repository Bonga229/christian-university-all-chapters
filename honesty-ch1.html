Chapter 1: What Is Honesty?

Honesty is not simply the absence of lies. It is the discipline of truth.

To be honest is to bring what is inside to the surface — without filters, without masks, without manipulation. Honesty is the foundation of trust, and trust is the foundation of every meaningful human relationship.

But honesty is dangerous. Because truth is heavy. It exposes. It reveals. It makes people uncomfortable.

And that is why most people fear it.

"The truth will set you free, but first it will piss you off." — Gloria Steinem

Biblical Roots of Honesty

In the Bible, lying is not just wrong — it is demonic.

"You belong to your father, the devil... When he lies, he speaks his native language, for he is a liar and the father of lies." — John 8:44

In contrast, truth is directly connected to the Spirit of God:

"But when he, the Spirit of truth, comes, he will guide you into all the truth." — John 16:13

Truth is not just a value. It is a Spirit.

Psychology of Truth

In psychology, honesty is linked to integrity — being whole and undivided inside. People who lie constantly often show higher anxiety, poor memory (because lies have to be maintained), and shallow relationships.

The brain knows when you’re fake. And so do other people.

We live in a world where image has replaced integrity. But humans are not fooled for long. Eventually, what is hidden comes to the surface.

"What’s done in darkness will be brought to light." — Luke 8:17

Then vs. Now

Let’s look at a story of the past:

There was a general in ancient Ethiopia named Gideon Yared. A powerful man. Respected. He was offered a seat in the royal court. But he turned it down.

Why?

Because the King had asked him to lie.

The nation had lost a major battle. The people were scared. The King wanted Yared to lie — to tell the nation they won, to keep the people calm.

But Yared refused.

"A lie is not peace. It is poison served in gold."

He told the people the truth. They mourned. They wept. But they reorganized. They prepared. And the next battle — they won.

Today, Yared is remembered in Alkebulan folklore as "the man who saved a nation by telling it the truth."

Now?

We lie to get jobs. We lie in our relationships. We lie to sell products. We lie to protect people — but really, we’re just protecting ourselves.

Truth is now seen as a weapon.

"Don’t be rude."
"That’s harsh."
"You can’t say that."

But here’s the thing:
Silence is not love.
Lies are not kindness.

Real Honesty Looks Like...

  • Telling your friend his idea needs work — not to bring him down, but to build him up.
  • Telling your girl what’s really bothering you — instead of going silent.
  • Telling yourself you’ve been lazy — not to shame yourself, but to change.

It’s not about being rude.
It’s about being real.

In Christian University...

We don’t sugarcoat. We don’t lie to fit in. We don’t hide truth to protect ego.

We choose to speak the truth — with love. Because in this university, our strength starts with our honesty.
