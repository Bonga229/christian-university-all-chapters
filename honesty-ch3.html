Chapter 3: The Truth Starts Within

The truth is simple: before you can be honest with the world, you have to be honest with yourself. That’s harder than it sounds, because lying to yourself is the easiest lie of all. You don’t need to say it out loud, you don’t need to prove it to anyone. You can just push it down, distract yourself, and pretend. But here’s the thing — ignoring truth doesn’t erase it. It just hides in the dark, shaping your actions without you even realizing it.

Think about the urge to post a picture online. Maybe it’s a picture where you’re half-naked, showing your body. On the surface, you might say, “I just felt like it” or “It’s just for fun.” But if you sit with yourself and strip away the excuses, the real reasons start to show. Maybe you want validation. Maybe the likes and comments give you a dopamine rush that makes you feel valuable. Maybe you grew up overlooked, and this is your way of finally being seen. Or maybe you were influenced without noticing — copying what you saw on Instagram, TV, or celebrities who made it look normal.

None of these motives make you a “bad” person. They make you human. But honesty means looking them in the eye without flinching.

I remember reflecting like this: “I want to post this picture because I crave validation. The truth is, I feel small when no one notices me. The likes give me a temporary high. That means, deep down, I’m living off the opinions of others.” That’s not a comfortable thought. In fact, it feels harsh, even insulting to myself. But at least it’s true. And with truth comes freedom. Because now the choice is real: Do I still post the picture, knowing that I’m chasing validation? Or do I stop, and face the insecurity instead of feeding it?

Honesty doesn’t force you into one path. It simply clears the fog so you can actually see where you’re standing. You may still decide to go ahead, or you may choose differently — but this time, you’ll know it’s your decision, not a lie you told yourself.

The truth starts within. And once you can be honest with yourself, no manipulation in the world can truly control you.
